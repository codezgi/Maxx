Suture Anchors

Suture Anchor Manufacturer in Türkiye: Maxx Global Medical

A suture anchor is an implant carrying surgical suture, used in arthroscopic surgery to reattach soft tissues such as tendons and ligaments to bone. Maxx Global Medical manufactures its suture anchors at its facility in İvedik OSB, Ankara, Türkiye, under an ISO 13485 certified quality management system, and exports to more than 25 countries. The product family covers titanium ELI (ASTM F136) screw anchors, radiolucent PEEK anchors, bone-preserving all-suture (soft) anchors and knotless pushlock systems; models are offered in a 2.0–5.0 mm diameter range with high-strength UHMWPE sutures in double-layer sterile packaging. Indication and usage decisions rest with healthcare professionals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a suture anchor?

A suture anchor is a small implant carrying surgical suture, used in arthroscopic and open surgery to reattach soft tissues such as tendons or ligaments to bone. Screw-in, all-suture (soft) and knotless types are available.

What materials are Maxx Global suture anchors made of?

Maxx Global Medical suture anchors are manufactured from Titanium ELI (ASTM F136), radiolucent PEEK and UHMWPE-based all-suture constructs; the sutures supplied with the anchors are high-strength UHMWPE.

Are you a suture anchor manufacturer in Türkiye?

Yes. Maxx Global Medical manufactures its suture anchors at its facility in İvedik OSB, Ankara, under an ISO 13485 certified quality management system, and exports to more than 25 countries.
